Biomedical Informatics & Training (BIT) Team

Institute for Genomic Medicine, Nationwide Children’s Hospital

Next-generation sequencing technologies have fundamentally changed the study of human genetics. Since the draft human genome was completed in 2001, thousands of disease-associated genes have been catalogued. Genetic testing has become a key diagnostic tool, especially for developmental disorders and rare genetic diseases.

Despite these advances, our understanding of genotype–phenotype relationships remains incomplete. Clinical genetic testing yields a molecular diagnosis in only 25–30% of referred patients, underscoring the need for deeper insight into the biological impact of thousands of genetic variants.

The incredible throughput and diverse applications of high-throughput sequencing instruments promise transformative discoveries. However, they also present significant bioinformatics challenges. The BIT team develops and deploys scalable computational tools to process raw genomic data, call and annotate variants, and prioritize candidates for further investigation.

Selected Projects

Structural Variant Pipeline

Clinical-grade pipeline for structural variant detection and interpretation from whole genome sequencing data.

WGS Structural Variants Rare Disease

  • Integrates multiple SV callers to improve sensitivity
  • Annotates genes, regulatory regions, and population databases
  • Supports cohort-scale reanalysis for rare disease discovery

Presented at: American Society of Human Genetics (ASHG), 2025

Impact: Enables improved detection and interpretation of structural variation in rare disease diagnostics, supporting clinical decision-making.

PacBio Long-Read WGS Pipeline

Long-read whole genome sequencing pipeline for improved structural variant detection and genome resolution in clinical and research settings.

PacBio Long-read SV Detection

  • Processes HiFi reads for high-accuracy genome analysis
  • Enables detection of complex structural variants missed by short reads
  • Supports scalable workflows on high-performance compute environments

Impact: Implemented a PacBio pipeline for internal use with our standard architecture style.

RNUniverse

Pipeline for annotation and prioritization of non-coding RNA genes in rare disease analysis.

ncRNA Annotation Rare Disease

  • Integrates multiple ncRNA databases and genomic annotations
  • Prioritizes regulatory and non-coding variants
  • Supports discovery beyond protein-coding regions

Impact: Expands diagnostic search space beyond traditional gene models.
